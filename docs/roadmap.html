Risks & dependencies

Top risks

HIGHUsability vs. rigor

The Research Edition is powerful but can intimidate. If "Pro mode" leaks complexity into the default path, casual users bounce. Mitigation: Simple-by-default, templates, onboarding (all in Now).

MED-HIEliciting correlations

Asking lay users for a correlation matrix is unrealistic. Mitigation: spike a simplified "these factors move together" toggle before committing engineering.

MEDElicitation friction

Ranges and uncertainty are extra work per estimate. Mitigation: smart defaults from confidence, templates pre-filled, calibration 2.0.

MEDCapacity vs. ambition

18 months is tight for a small team. Later is deliberately directional. Protect a 70/20/10 split and apply the "what comes off?" rule before adding anything.

Critical dependency chain

  • Unify editions → Calibration 2.0, Sensitivity 2.0 (engine must be one codebase first)
  • Accounts & sync → Team decisions → Pro tier (the whole monetization line)
  • Decision record / reports → Pro tier, integrations (shareable artifact drives growth + paid value)
  • Calibration 2.0 → Decision journal (outcomes feed personal calibration)
  • Correlated criteria + advanced preferences — highest technical risk; gate on a research spike

Changes this update

v1.0 — initial baseline

  • Consolidated the two shipped prototypes (simple scorer + Monte-Carlo engine) into a single product strategy with a Simple/Pro split.
  • Pulled Decision record & reports forward into early Next — RICE flagged it as a near-quick-win (3000, the top score).
  • Moved the PhD+ features (correlated criteria, prospect theory, AHP) from "next engineering work" to Later as bets pending a research spike — low RICE confidence didn't justify committing.
  • Sequenced accounts & sync ahead of the team/monetization line, since it blocks everything shared and paid.

Capacity note. Roadmap is zero-sum against the team. Target allocation: 70% roadmap features · 20% technical health (the unified engine pays this down) · 10% buffer. Now and Next are commitments; Later is a set of directional bets, not dated promises. Before anything new enters Now, something moves out — that's the discipline this plan runs on.
